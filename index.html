Thomas Vanderstichele

I am currently a third year PhD student in the Human Genetics department at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute and the University of Cambridge. My supervisors are Dr. Emma Davenport and Dr Leopold Parts. My research focuses on the interpretation of rare genetic variation using large-scale whole genome sequencing and bulk RNA sequencing datasets.

I obtained a first class degree in Biochemistry from the University of Oxford. For my Master's dissertation I conducted research at Princetion University in the lab of Professor Mona Singh. This project focused on developing probabilistic machine learning methods to predict non-canonical DNA-binding modes of zinc finger proteins. For one summer, I also worked in the lab of Professor Phil Biggin at the University of Oxford researching the structure of nicotinic acetylcholine receptors.
